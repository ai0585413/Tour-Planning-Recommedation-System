Top Destination For Your Next Vacation

Explore the Vibrant Culture and Natural Wonders of Bangladeshi Upazilas

Lalbagh Fort

Lalbagh Fort, located in Old Dhaka, is a historic Mughal fort known for its beautiful gardens, impressive architecture, and rich history. It attracts tourists with its stunning mosque, tomb, and museum, offering a glimpse into Bangladesh's Mughal past.

Bangladesh National Parliament House

The Bangladesh National Parliament House, also known as Jatiyo Sangsad Bhaban, is an iconic architectural masterpiece located in Sher-e-Bangla Nagar, Dhaka. It attracts tourists for its unique blend of modernist architecture and serene surroundings.

Sonargaon Museum

Sonargaon Museum, located in Narayanganj, showcases Bangladesh's rich cultural heritage with exhibits of traditional art, crafts, and ancient artifacts. It draws tourists with its historical significance and displays of folk arts, including the famous muslin textiles.

National Martyrs' Monument

The National Martyrs' Monument, or Jatiyo Smriti Soudho, located in Savar near Dhaka, symbolizes Bangladesh's independence and honors those who sacrificed their lives in the 1971 Liberation War. Tourists are drawn to its striking architecture and serene surroundings, reflecting the nation's pride.

Central Shaheed Minar

The Central Shaheed Minar, located near Dhaka University, honors the martyrs of the 1952 Language Movement. It attracts tourists for its historical significance as a symbol of the fight for Bengali language rights.

Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, located in the Khulna Division of southwestern Bangladesh, is the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is renowned for its unique biodiversity, especially the endangered Bengal tiger, attracting tourists for wildlife watching and boat tours.

SandMartin

The Sand Martin is found along rivers and wetlands, particularly in areas like Cox's Bazar, where it is known for its unique nesting behavior of digging tunnels in sandy banks. This migratory bird attracts birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts, making it a popular spot for wildlife observation during the breeding season.
